Earth Science

By DataHub Team • 45 lessons • 20 datasets

Explore Earth's systems through the lens of data science. Students will analyze real-world datasets to understand weather patterns, climate change, geological processes, and environmental science while building essential data literacy skills. Each lesson aligns with NGSS standards and provides scaffolded data science instruction throughout the academic year, following the Data Science Learning Progressions framework.

Earthquake Pattern Investigation

Using real earthquake datasets to identify plate boundary patterns and relationships between magnitude, depth, and location.

In this lesson students will explore a global earthquake dataset to identify spatio-temporal patterns, compare shallow versus deep earthquakes, and examine how earthquake magnitudes relate to plate boundary types. Activities include mapping events, creating summaries (mean, median, distribution), and designing short investigations using filters and simple visualizations.

Lesson at a glance
Grade
6-8 (Middle School)
Time
2-3 class periods
Skills
Mapping, Filtering, Summarizing
Standards
NGSS MS-ESS2-2, MS-ESS2-3
What Students Learn
  • How tectonic plates move and interact at boundaries
  • Why earthquakes occur in specific global patterns
  • The relationship between earthquake depth and tectonic settings
Success Indicators
  • Students can identify plate boundaries on a map
  • Students can predict earthquake locations based on plate tectonics
  • Students can use evidence to support scientific claims

Standards & Skills Alignment

Science Content Standards
NGSS Middle School Earth Science
  • MS-ESS2-2: Construct an explanation based on evidence for how geoscience processes have changed Earth's surface
  • MS-ESS2-3: Analyze and interpret data on the distribution of fossils and rocks, continental shapes, and seafloor structures to provide evidence of past plate motions
  • Science & Engineering Practice: Analyzing and Interpreting Data
Data Science Learning Progressions
Skills students will develop
  • Data Collection: Accessing and understanding real scientific datasets
  • Visualization: Creating maps to show spatial patterns
  • Analysis: Using filters and summary statistics to identify relationships
  • Communication: Drawing evidence-based conclusions from data
Teacher Support & Prerequisites

Technology Requirements

Minimal tech needed! Students can complete this lesson using:

  • Computers with internet access (1 per pair of students)
  • Spreadsheet software (Google Sheets or Excel)
  • Our web-based mapping tool (no installation required)

No-Tech Alternative

Limited access to computers? We've included printable maps and data cards that students can use to complete a modified version of this activity.

Differentiation & Teaching Tips

Supporting All Learners

  • Struggling students: Pre-filter the dataset to show fewer points
  • Advanced students: Challenge them to investigate specific regions or explore depth/magnitude correlations
  • ELL support: Visual vocabulary cards and sentence frames included

Common Misconceptions

  • Students may confuse earthquake magnitude with intensity
  • Many assume earthquakes only happen at plate boundaries
  • Students often think all fault lines produce the same types of earthquakes
