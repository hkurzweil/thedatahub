Earth Science

By DataHub Team • 45 lessons • 20 datasets

Explore Earth's systems through the lens of data science. Students will analyze real-world datasets to understand weather patterns, climate change, geological processes, and environmental science while building essential data literacy skills. Each lesson aligns with NGSS standards and provides scaffolded data science instruction throughout the academic year, following the Data Science Learning Progressions framework.

Earthquake Pattern Investigation

Using real earthquake datasets to identify plate boundary patterns and relationships between magnitude, depth, and location.

In this lesson students will explore a global earthquake dataset to identify spatio-temporal patterns, compare shallow versus deep earthquakes, and examine how earthquake magnitudes relate to plate boundary types. Activities include mapping events, creating summaries (mean, median, distribution), and designing short investigations using filters and simple visualizations.

Lesson at a glance
Grade
8-10
Time
2-3 class periods
Skills
Mapping, Filtering, Summarizing
Standards
NGSS ESS2, HS-ESS2
What students do
  • Map recent global earthquake events
  • Compare shallow vs deep event distributions
  • Investigate magnitude vs depth relationships
Teacher notes

Prep: download dataset and open mapping tool. Have students work in pairs for mapping and produce a short write-up of findings.
